Plumbing

Plumbing contractors are often the first to arrive at a project and the last to leave. Sagamore realizes this, and works diligently to set a standard for others to follow. It's a "professional" approach to a blue-collar industry that sets us apart from the others.

From the beginning, Sagamore's planners and estimators pride themselves on accuracy while looking for ways to reduce project costs for owners and developers. Our CAD team takes this further by optimizing coordination and off-site fabrication—reducing the number of bodies on a general contractor's site. In the field, plumbers who have benefited from a comprehensive in-house job and safety training are qualified for every aspect of construction plumbing—including plumbing for medical, laboratory and biohazard gases and fluids. We self-perform all plumbing functions to maximize quality control.

The totality of these efforts are summarized by our core principles: Client satisfaction and quality craftsmanship.

Service Contracts

A preventative maintenance agreement for your facility's plumbing/HVAC systems provides peace of mind for your managers, reduced downtime due to unexpected failures, and longevity for the equipment components of your systems.

Sagamore's service department will custom-design a cost-efficient program that fits your organization's unique needs. Typical coverage might include preventative maintenance inspections 1-4 times annually and intermediate filter changes. Contracts can be written for from 1 to 10 years.

Our technicians are certified for most major brands: Carrier, Amana, York, Trane, American Standard, Bryant, A.O. Smith, Viessman, Burnham and Kohler. We provide private label programs to target specific needs of our customers.

Sagamore can also provide warranty, extended warranty, and optional warranty programs for OEM customers throughout New England. These OEM programs range from 1st-day through 5th-year coverage, to add-on labor and extended parts coverage.

Our Story

In 1991 Joe Harold set up a small plumbing shop in the 400-square-foot basement of a small office building in Sagamore Beach, Massachusetts. Even with just two employees, the mission was the same as today: an unyielding commitment to customer satisfaction and to job quality.

Commercial and industrial plumbing was the young company's strength—especially in the construction field. As more and more contractors gained trust in Sagamore, the company moved to Weymouth in 1994 and added residential and light commercial plumbing and HVAC to its services. Soon after Sagamore developed a reputation for proficiency in plumbing for medical and laboratory gases.

After 18 years of steady growth, Sagamore today is a diversified plumbing and HVAC company employing a staff of over 150 individuals serving contractors and businesses as a dedicated team. The company now operates out of a 25,000-square-foot facility with a full-time fabrication shop, a dedicated staff training center and a fleet of over 35 vehicles.
